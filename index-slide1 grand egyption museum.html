grand egyptian museum

The Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM) is one of the largest and most important archaeological museums in the world. Located near the Giza Pyramids, it is designed to showcase Egypt’s rich ancient history in a modern and innovative way. The museum brings together thousands of artifacts that tell the story of Egyptian civilization, from prehistoric times through the Greco-Roman period, offering visitors a unique cultural and educational experience.

the creation of the grand egyptian museum(GEM)

Inside the Grand Egyptian Museum, the places are organized to guide visitors through Egypt’s history in a clear and detailed way. The Grand Hall welcomes visitors with the massive statue of King Ramses II, leading to the Grand Staircase, which displays large statues, sarcophagi, and architectural pieces arranged by theme and era. The Main Galleries consist of multiple halls that present artifacts chronologically, covering prehistoric Egypt, the Old, Middle, and New Kingdoms, and the Greco-Roman period. One of the most important areas is the Tutankhamun Galleries, which display the complete collection of treasures from his tomb for the first time in one place. The museum also includes the Khufu Solar Boat Museum, conservation and restoration laboratories, a Children’s Museum, temporary exhibition halls, and visitor facilities such as restaurants, shops, and cultural spaces.
